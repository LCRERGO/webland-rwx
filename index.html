Introduction to Linux

History

Linux was created by Linus Torvalds during college while wanting to improve on Minix, which his professor had created.

Controversy

Richard Stallman, creator of the GNU utilities wanted to have Linux always called by the name GNU/Linux (despite the unpronouncable mothfull that is). This name was quickly dropped due to covenience and lack of consideration for the thousands of other contributors from other projects outside of the GNU project.
